================================================================================
  1. GAME OVERVIEW
================================================================================

UPHOLSTERY! is a Roblox craft-simulation game where players run their own
upholstery business. You accept client jobs, travel to their location, repair
their furniture through skill-based minigames, and grow your reputation and
following as a craftsperson.

The fantasy is progression from unknown apprentice to sought-after elite
craftsperson — where your reputation determines what clients reach out to you
directly, your follower count reflects your public profile, your money funds
better tools, and your skill determines whether you can actually deliver.


DESIGN PILLARS
--------------
  Skill has a ceiling, loadout raises the floor.
    Minigames are genuinely hard. Tools make them more manageable, not trivial.

  Every job is a bet.
    Accepting a high-tier job is a calculated risk. Fail it and you pay — in
    rep, in wasted tools, in time.

  Reputation is earned, not bought.
    Money buys tools. Reputation comes from actually doing the work well.

  The board is shared, the grind is personal.
    All players compete for the same public jobs. Your Direct Offers are yours
    alone.


================================================================================
  2. CORE GAMEPLAY LOOP
================================================================================

  Open laptop
    -> Browse shared job board + check Direct Offers
    -> Set Toolbox loadout for the job
    -> Accept job
    -> Teleport to client location
    -> Complete minigame chain (repair the furniture)
    -> Exit via door -> result submitted
    -> Earn money + followers + rep  (or lose rep on fail)
    -> Spend money on perk charges
    -> Repeat with better loadout

The loop has two distinct rhythms:

  Short loop (per job):    Accept -> play -> earn. 3-8 minutes per tier.
  Long loop (per session): Build rep -> unlock better Direct Offers -> earn
                           more -> afford better perks -> take harder jobs.


================================================================================
  3. JOB SYSTEM
================================================================================

3.1  SHARED JOB BOARD
---------------------
All players on the server see the same job board. Jobs are generated
server-side and refreshed every 15-30 seconds. This is first-come,
first-served — if another player grabs a job before you click Accept,
it disappears.

There are NO reputation gates on the shared board. Any player can attempt
any job they see. High-tier jobs being rare on the board is the natural
filter, not an artificial lock.

JOB TIERS AND BOARD RARITY

  Tier        Pay Range           Difficulty  Fragile  Approx. Frequency
  ----------  ------------------  ----------  -------  -------------------
  Starter     $50    - $150       Easy        No       ~5 per refresh
  Mid         $200   - $500       Medium      No       ~2.8 per refresh
  Premium     $600   - $1,200     Hard        Yes      ~1.3 per refresh
  Prestige    $1,500 - $3,000     Hard        Yes      ~0.6 per refresh
  Exclusive   $3,500 - $7,000     Hard        Yes      ~0.2 per refresh
  Elite       $7,500 - $15,000    Hard        Yes      ~1 per 14 refreshes

Elite jobs are genuine lottery moments on the shared board, appearing
roughly once every 8-16 minutes at best. They are not a reliable income
source from the board — that comes from Direct Offers.

Special is not a board tier. See Section 13.

BOARD POOL SIZE
  Solo server:    minimum 4 jobs
  Full server:    up to 12 jobs
  Scales at 1.5-2.5 jobs per player


3.2  DIRECT OFFERS
------------------
Direct Offers are personal job invitations generated per-player based on
reputation. They appear in a dedicated section of the MoneyMaker tab,
separate from the shared board.

  - Direct Offers do NOT draw from the shared pool — generated independently.
  - Each offer has a 5-minute expiry timer. Ignore it and it is gone.
  - Offer quality and frequency scale with reputation tier.
  - Only the player they were sent to can accept them.

Thematic justification: clients who have seen your Talkogram posts or were
referred by past customers contact you directly. Higher reputation means
better clients — and more of them.

DIRECT OFFER TIERS BY REPUTATION

  Rep Range    Offer Tier Range     Concurrent  Refresh Interval
  -----------  -------------------  ----------  ----------------
  0.0 - 0.9   None                 --          --
  1.0 - 1.9   Mid - Premium        1           4 minutes
  2.0 - 3.4   Premium - Exclusive  2           3 minutes
  3.5 - 4.4   Exclusive - Elite    2           2.5 minutes
  4.5 - 5.0   Elite - Special      3           2 minutes


3.3  JOB STRUCTURE
------------------
Each job contains:
  - Tier             determines pay range, difficulty, furniture count
  - Furniture pieces 1-3 items depending on tier
  - Minigame chain   one minigame per furniture piece (or chain for single-piece)
  - Fragile modifier tightens all precision windows by 40% (multiplier: 0.6)
  - Flavor text      one-line description that sets the scene

PER-TIER JOB STRUCTURE

  Tier       Furniture  Minigames  Difficulty  Fragile
  ---------  ---------  ---------  ----------  -------
  Starter    1          1          Easy        No
  Mid        1-2        1-2        Medium      No
  Premium    2          2-3        Hard        Yes
  Prestige   2-3        3-4        Hard        Yes
  Exclusive  2-3        4-5        Hard        Yes
  Elite      3          5-6        Hard        Yes

Special event jobs are defined per event — see Section 13.

Higher tiers do not increase difficulty beyond Hard — instead they
increase the number of minigames in the chain. Staying consistent
across 6-8 minigames is the real challenge at Elite and Special.


MINIGAME POOL BY TIER

Each tier draws only from its available minigame pool. Higher tiers
inherit all lower-tier minigames and add new ones.

  Tier       Available Minigames
  ---------  -------------------------------------------------------
  Starter    Tack Removal, Staple Gunning, Seam Stitching,
             Foam Padding
  Mid        (same as Starter)
  Premium    + Button Tufting                       [POST-LAUNCH]
  Prestige   + Spring Tensioning                    [POST-LAUNCH]
  Exclusive  + Webbing Weave                        [POST-LAUNCH]
  Elite      + Corner Fold                          [POST-LAUNCH]

Special events define their own minigame pool per event — see Section 13.

Alpha launches with 4 base minigames. Post-launch updates introduce
one new minigame per tier starting at Premium, each with matching
tier-gated perks (see section 6.5).


3.4  HOT JOBS
-------------
Hot Jobs are special board events appearing every 5-8 minutes. A 10-second
countdown plays on the board before the job becomes claimable, alerting all
players at the same time.

  Pay multiplier:  3.0x normal tier pay
  Follower bonus:  +75%
  Visual:          Animated gold border on the job card

The countdown creates shared anticipation — everyone on the server sees it
and whoever clicks fastest wins. See section 12 for full Hot Job rules.


================================================================================
  4. MINIGAMES
================================================================================

All minigames share a common interface:
  Start(container, config) -> GetResult() -> Cleanup()

Results in ascending order:  FAIL -> PASS -> GOOD -> PERFECT

In a minigame chain, the worst result across all games is the final result.


4.1  TACK REMOVAL  (osu!-style timing)
---------------------------------------
Circular tacks appear on screen with a shrinking approach ring. Click the
tack when the ring reaches the center. Miss timing = miss point.

  Parameter          Easy    Medium  Hard
  -----------------  ------  ------  ------
  Tack count         4-7     8-14    12-18
  Approach time      2.6s    2.0s    1.4s
  Perfect threshold  65%     72%     82%
  Good threshold     35%     42%     52%


4.2  STAPLE GUNNING  (bar timing)
-----------------------------------
A bar moves horizontally across a track with nested Good and Perfect zones.
Click when the bar is inside the zone. Bar speeds up with successive hits.

  Parameter          Easy    Medium  Hard
  -----------------  ------  ------  ------
  Staple count       3-5     6-10    9-14
  Bar speed          0.45    0.62    0.85
  Good zone width    0.15    0.11    0.08
  Perfect zone width 0.07    0.05    0.035
  Speed ramp-up      0.040   0.040   0.060


4.3  SEAM STITCHING  (free-drag path)
---------------------------------------
Player drags a needle along a zig-zag path. Deviation is tracked
continuously. Green trail = on path, red trail = off path.

  Parameter              Easy    Medium  Hard
  ---------------------  ------  ------  ------
  Segments               3-4     5-7     8-12
  Time per segment       12s     9s      7s
  Deviation tolerance    48      32      20
  Perfect max deviation  35%     35%     35%
  Good max deviation     70%     70%     70%


4.4  FOAM PADDING  (grid puzzle)
----------------------------------
A RE4-inspired grid puzzle. Place Tetris-like foam pieces to fill a grid.
Pieces can be rotated. Score based on grid coverage.

  Parameter          Easy    Medium  Hard
  -----------------  ------  ------  ------
  Piece count        3       5       7
  Time limit         65s     50s     35s
  Perfect threshold  100%    100%    100%
  Good threshold     67%     75%     80%


4.5  BUTTON TUFTING  (memory sequence)  [POST-LAUNCH]
-----------------------------------------------------
A grid of buttons lights up in a sequence. Recreate the sequence by
clicking the buttons in the same order. Wrong click = failed round.

  Parameter          Easy    Medium  Hard
  -----------------  ------  ------  ------
  Sequence length    3       5       7
  Display time (s)   2.4     1.8     1.2
  Rounds             2       3       4


4.6  SPRING TENSIONING  (hold / release)  [POST-LAUNCH]
-------------------------------------------------------
A needle rests on a pressure gauge. Hold a button to build tension;
release when the needle lands in the green zone. Overshoot and the
needle bounces past — you must wait for it to settle and try again.

  Parameter           Easy    Medium  Hard
  ------------------  ------  ------  ------
  Green zone width    0.28    0.18    0.11
  Needle drift speed  0.30    0.50    0.72
  Attempts allowed    4       3       2


4.7  WEBBING WEAVE  (logic puzzle)  [POST-LAUNCH]
-------------------------------------------------
Drag webbing strips across a frame in the correct over-under
alternating pattern. Each strip must correctly pass over or under
every crossing strip. Wrong placement highlights in red and must
be re-placed.

  Parameter       Easy    Medium  Hard
  --------------  ------  ------  ------
  Strip count     4       6       8
  Time limit (s)  60      45      30


4.8  CORNER FOLD  (ordered steps)  [POST-LAUNCH]
------------------------------------------------
Fold fabric corners by clicking fold lines in the correct order.
Each fold reveals the next. Clicking a fold out of order resets
the current corner.

  Parameter               Easy    Medium  Hard
  ----------------------  ------  ------  ------
  Corners per piece       2       3       4
  Hint visibility (s)     3.0     1.5     0.5
  Time per corner (s)     20      14      9


4.9  THE FRAGILE MODIFIER
--------------------------
When a job is marked Fragile, all precision windows across all minigames
tighten by 40% (multiplier: 0.6). Applies to:

  - Good/Perfect zone widths in StapleGunning
  - Deviation tolerance in SeamStitching
  - Snap tolerance in FoamPadding

Fragile applies to all tiers Premium and above.
The Fragile Specialist perk softens this modifier.


================================================================================
  5. REPUTATION SYSTEM
================================================================================

Reputation is a floating-point value from 0.0 to 5.0, starting at 0.0.
It represents the player's professional standing as an upholsterer.

Reputation is NOT a gate on the shared job board. It drives:
  1. Direct Offer quality  — higher rep = better personal invitations
  2. Toolbox slot progress — rep milestones unlock additional perk slots


5.1  REPUTATION GAINS
---------------------
  Result   Change
  -------  ------
  Perfect  +0.20
  Good     +0.10
  Pass     +0.05
  Fail     Scaled by tier (see section 11)
  Abandon  Scaled by tier (see section 11)

Rep cannot go below 0.0.


5.2  REP MILESTONES
-------------------
  Rep    Milestone
  -----  -------------------------------------------------
  1.0    Direct Offers begin (Mid-Premium range)
  1.5    4th Toolbox slot unlocked
  2.0    Direct Offers upgrade (Premium-Exclusive)
  3.0    5th Toolbox slot unlocked
  3.5    Direct Offers upgrade (Exclusive-Elite)
  4.5    Direct Offers upgrade (Elite-Special)
  5.0    Max rep — cosmetic milestone, max Direct Offer quality


================================================================================
  6. TOOLBOX — PICK 5 PERK SYSTEM
================================================================================

The Toolbox is the player's loadout. It holds up to 5 perk slots, each filled
with a single charged perk. Players configure it before accepting jobs.

Perk charges are consumed on every job attempt, regardless of success or
failure. Failing a job wastes your charges. This is intentional — it creates
a real cost to overreaching.

Example loadouts:
  Specialist:   5x Curved Needle -> extreme SeamStitching ease, no economy perks
  Profit build: 3x Premium Invoice (Bespoke) + Safety Net + Word of Mouth
  Risk hedge:   2x Foam Press Kit + 2x Safety Net + Damage Waiver


6.1  SLOT PROGRESSION
---------------------
  Slots  Condition
  -----  -----------------
  3      Starting
  4      Reach rep 1.5
  5      Reach rep 3.0

Starting with 3 slots avoids overwhelming new players before they understand
the system. Unlocking slots is a tangible, exciting rep milestone.


6.2  PERK TIERS
---------------
Every perk comes in two tiers:

  BOLT     Accessible, affordable, reliable. Lower effect, no conditions.
           Available from the shop for in-game money.

  BESPOKE  Stronger effect, often conditional. May require a rep threshold
           to purchase or drop from job completions. Many have conditions
           like "only on Good or better" that reward skilled play.

Bespoke is not always strictly better. A conditional Bespoke perk used
carelessly may underperform a reliable Bolt perk.


6.3  PERK ACQUISITION
---------------------
  Source                   Perks Available
  -----------------------  --------------------------------------------------
  Shop (money)             All minigame tools, basic boosters — main spend
  Job completion drops     Protection and board perks — reward for playing
  Rep milestones           Rare and Bespoke perks — prestige unlocks

The first time a player completes a Rare+ job, they receive a free Bolt
perk as a tutorial reward that teaches the Toolbox system through play.

----------------
Money is spent primarily on Toolbox perk charges. This keeps high earners
continuously spending rather than accumulating idle wealth.

Primary sinks:
  - Perk charges (Bolt and Bespoke, all categories)
  - Shop utility items

The rich spend to stay competitive. A player who hoards money but skips
tool investment is at a consistent disadvantage on Hard + Fragile jobs.

================================================================================
  9. TALKOGRAM  [POST-LAUNCH]
================================================================================

Talkogram is the in-game activity feed accessed through the laptop.

It is a lightweight server-local feed — not a full social media platform.
There are no comments, no likes, no cross-server persistence. It exists to
give job completions a moment of acknowledgment and to give the server a
shared sense of activity.


9.1  WHAT IT DOES
-----------------
After every completed job (Pass, Good, or Perfect), a post is automatically
generated and added to the server feed. The post is visible to all players
on the same server.

Posts use templates based on result quality:

  Result   Template
  -------  --------------------------------------------------------
  Perfect  "Absolutely nailed this [job name]. Client loved it."
  Good     "Pretty happy with how this [job name] turned out."
  Pass     "Just finished a [job name] — not my best, but done!"

Posts include: player name, job name, result, and timestamp.
Failed jobs do not generate a post.


9.2  WHAT IT DOES NOT DO
------------------------
  - No comments
  - No likes
  - No cross-server DataStore feed
  - No popular post tracking
  - No per-player post limits

The feed is session-scoped. When the server closes, the feed clears.


9.3  WHY IT EXISTS
------------------
Completing a job should feel witnessed. Without the feed, you watch numbers
go up — money, followers, rep — but nothing in the world acknowledges the
work. The Talkogram post is the "ding" moment: a line appears, the server
sees what you just did, and it feels like it mattered.

It also gives followers their thematic context. Players earn followers because
their completed jobs appear on Talkogram. Without that story, followers would
be an unexplained number.


================================================================================
  10. PLAYER PROGRESSION
================================================================================

Progression is multi-axis — players grow along money, followers, and
reputation simultaneously. Each axis drives different systems.

  Money       -> House upgrades (one of two requirements)
  Followers   -> House upgrades (second requirement; rewards quality play)
  Reputation  -> Direct Offer quality + Toolbox slot unlocks


10.1  PLAYER HOUSE  [POST-LAUNCH]
----------------------------------
The player's house upgrades when BOTH money and follower thresholds are met.
Requiring followers alongside money means players who play well (earning
Good/Perfect results) progress faster than players who grind low-tier jobs
for raw cash.

  House                Money      Followers   Description
  -------------------  ---------  ----------  -------------------------------
  Starter Flat         $0         0            Basic starting apartment
  Corner House         $5,000     1,000        Modest street-level workshop
  Workshop Villa       $75,000    15,000       Dedicated professional space
  Skyline Penthouse    $750,000   125,000      Elite craftsperson's residence

Houses are visual status symbols and serve as the player's spawn point.


10.2  REPUTATION MILESTONES (SUMMARY)
--------------------------------------
  Rep    Reward
  -----  ----------------------------------------
  1.0    Direct Offers begin
  1.5    4th Toolbox slot
  2.0    Direct Offer tier upgrade
  3.0    5th Toolbox slot
  3.5    Direct Offer tier upgrade
  4.5    Elite-Special Direct Offers
  5.0    Max rep cosmetic badge


10.3  DAILY CONTRACTS  (planned)
---------------------------------
A separate daily board shows 3 rotating challenges. Examples:

  "Complete 4 Rare or higher jobs today           -> $500 bonus"
  "Achieve Perfect on 2 Fragile jobs              -> 1 Bespoke perk charge"
  "Earn $3,000 in a single session                -> Word of Mouth (Bespoke)"

Daily contracts give a login motivation independent of the board state.
They refresh at midnight server time.


================================================================================
  11. PENALTY SYSTEM
================================================================================

Failure and abandonment carry rep consequences that scale with tier.

Why scaling: a new player gambling on an Elite board job has little rep to
protect — the penalty barely hurts. A veteran at rep 4.0 failing that same
job loses meaningful progress. The system is self-calibrating without needing
explicit gates.


11.1  REPUTATION PENALTIES BY TIER
------------------------------------
  Tier       Fail     Abandon
  ---------  -------  -------
  Starter    -0.03    -0.05
  Mid        -0.05    -0.08
  Premium    -0.10    -0.15
  Prestige   -0.15    -0.22
  Exclusive  -0.22    -0.32
  Elite      -0.30    -0.45

Special event penalties are defined per event — see Section 13.


11.2  COMPOUND FAILURE COST
----------------------------
Failing a job costs the player four things at once:

  1. Rep loss           scaled by tier (see above)
  2. Tool charges lost  all equipped Toolbox perks lose one charge
  3. No pay earned      time spent, nothing received
  4. Time lost          another job could have been taken instead

There is NO direct money deduction on failure. Tool charge burn already
functions as the money penalty — it scales with how much the player invested
in their loadout. The riskier and better-equipped the attempt, the more it
costs to fail.

Failed jobs also do not generate a Talkogram post, meaning no followers
are earned. On high-tier jobs where follower gains are large, this is a
meaningful additional cost felt in house upgrade progress.


11.3  INFORMED RISK
-------------------
Every job card shows before acceptance:
  - Difficulty level   (Easy / Medium / Hard)
  - Fragile indicator  (if applicable)
  - Minigame count     (number in chain)
  - Rep penalty        (exact rep loss on fail)

Players make an informed bet. If they fail after seeing the consequences,
the punishment feels earned, not arbitrary.


================================================================================
  12. HOT JOBS
================================================================================

Hot Jobs are special board events appearing every 5-8 minutes. A 10-second
animated countdown plays before the job becomes claimable, alerting the
entire server simultaneously.

  Pay multiplier:     3.0x the tier's normal range
  Follower bonus:     +75%
  Visual:             Animated gold border on the job card
  Countdown:          10 seconds, visible to all players

  Job Alert (Bespoke) perk: gives a 5-second early notification before the
  countdown starts, allowing prepared players to position themselves.

Hot Jobs are the most exciting shared board moments. Everyone on the server
sees the same countdown and whoever clicks Accept fastest wins.

Hot Job Hunter perk can increase the multiplier to 3.5x (Bolt) or 4.0x
(Bespoke) for equipped players.


================================================================================
  13. SPECIAL EVENTS
================================================================================

Special Events are limited-time modes tied to real-world calendar events
(Christmas, Halloween, etc.). They are not a permanent job tier — they
activate and deactivate on a schedule defined by the development team.

All parameters are variable and defined per event:

  Parameter         Notes
  ----------------  ----------------------------------------------------------
  Pay range         Set per event. Can exceed Elite range or be intentionally
                    lower for accessibility events.
  Difficulty        Set per event. Can be Easy (fun/festive) or Hard
                    (limited-time challenge).
  Fragile modifier  Optional. Applied or omitted per event design.
  Minigame pool     Custom per event. May include themed variants of base
                    minigames or entirely new one-off minigames.
  Follower gains    Set per event.
  Penalty scaling   Set per event. Accessibility events may reduce penalties.
  Duration          Active window defined per event (e.g. December 1–31).

Special Event jobs appear in a dedicated section of the job board or
replace a portion of the standard board during the event window. The
exact presentation is decided per event.

Special Events are not designed during alpha. Parameters will be
authored in a per-event config when each event is developed.

================================================================================
  APPENDIX B — BALANCE NOTES
================================================================================

  - Rep gains are intentionally slow. 25 Perfect jobs to reach max. Rep is a
    long-term stat that reflects a real session history.

  - Elite/Special jobs on the shared board are rare enough (~1 per 8-16 min)
    to feel like events. Direct Offers are the veteran's reliable pipeline.

  - Bespoke perk conditions ("Good or better only") mean skilled players
    benefit more from the same perk than unskilled players. The system
    rewards competence, not just spending.

  - Perk charge costs should be balanced so that a full Toolbox loadout for
    a Special job costs roughly 10-15% of that job's expected pay on success.
    Investment should feel significant but not punishing.

  - The abandon penalty is always ~1.5x the fail penalty. Abandoning a job
    mid-task leaves the client hanging entirely, while a failed attempt at
    least made the effort.

  - House upgrade follower thresholds are intentionally harder to hit than
    money thresholds at higher tiers. The Skyline Penthouse requires 125,000
    followers — achievable only through sustained Good/Perfect play on
    mid-to-high tier jobs. It should feel like a genuine milestone, not an
    inevitable grind endpoint.

  - Failed jobs produce no Talkogram post and therefore no followers. On
    Special jobs (800-1,200 followers per completion), a single failure
    represents a significant setback in house upgrade progress. This is
    intended — it adds weight to high-stakes attempts beyond rep loss alone.
